Twitter Mentions for Both Candidates

Social media are known to be influencial over the U.S. political process. Here shows the collection of mentions of both of the candidate on Twitter from October 15 2020 to October 25 2020. Notice the surge of mentions on October 23 2020 for both parties, the Presidential Debate took place on that day.

Chance of Winning (State)

This is the prediction of chances of winning for each state from The Economist. The model demonstrates that the closer to 100%, chances for Biden to win it higher, and contrary closer to 0% means Trump has a higher chance to win. Whereas closer to 50% are the unpredictable or neutral state.

Polling Average in Whole

This is the collection of polling average collectively from March 2020 to Oct 2020 nationwide.

We could observed the decrease on the supporting rate for Trump starting from March, where the COVID-19 outbreak are starting the get out of control.

As for June to July is where the social unrest #blacklivesmatter protest happened, this is when the supporting rate for Trump has drop significantly.

Electoral College Votes

An electoral college is a set of electors who are selected to elect a candidate to particular offices. Americans have to cast their ballots for the electors from their state.

This time around there are 538 electors and either of the candidates have to get 270 votes or more from the electors to win.
